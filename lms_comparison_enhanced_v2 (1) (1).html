Feature-by-Feature Explanation — All 16 Features
Detailed breakdown of every feature requirement with platform-level capability ratings and real IndiaMART T&D journey context. Features marked with a star (★) are the ones most critical to IndiaMART's unique training architecture.
Category 1 · Content & Tracking
📡
Feature 1 · xAPI over SCORM Tracking ★
Detailed step-by-step behaviour capture
xAPI (Experience API) logs granular learning statements like "Rohan answered Q3 of the Lead Management quiz incorrectly" or "OJT module paused at 4:32 min", unlike SCORM which only records a binary pass/fail. This is critical for IndiaMART's NSD field sales OJT (30 days) where reps shadow buddies across multiple modules — xAPI captures every interaction step, not just whether a course was "completed."

Why partial platforms fall short: Workday/Sana is architected around its own AI tracking model rather than xAPI, so granular xAPI statements for external SCORM packages are inconsistently reported — the L&D team wouldn't get the per-module dropout data they need from OJT modules. Learnyst/Graphy have no xAPI support at all, making deep tracking impossible.

IndiaMART example: During the 30-day NSD OJT, an L&D admin notices via xAPI data that 78% of reps in the Pune branch are replaying the "RCA Process" module more than twice. This signals the module needs simplification — actionable insight impossible with SCORM alone.
✓ SAP Litmos ✓ Docebo ✓ Cornerstone OD ✓ Absorb LMS ✓ SAP SuccessFactors ✓ Cornerstone LXP ✓ TalentLMS ✓ Disprz ~ Workday/Sana ✗ Learnyst/Graphy
📶
Feature 2 · Offline Sync ★★
Mobile offline access + data sync on reconnect
IndiaMART's NSD field sales team is the largest sales vertical, covering Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities across India where mobile internet is unreliable. An LMS must allow reps to pre-download training modules and access them offline during OJT field visits, then auto-sync completion data once connectivity returns.

Why most platforms are partial: Docebo, Cornerstone, Absorb, SAP Litmos, SAP SuccessFactors, Cornerstone LXP, TalentLMS all have mobile apps that can cache some content, but full xAPI sync upon reconnect is not guaranteed — some only mark courses as "completed" without saving granular interaction data. This is a significant gap for OJT tracking.

Why Workday/Sana is a hard no: As of 2026, Workday Learning/Sana operates entirely cloud-native with no offline mode. A field sales rep in a low-connectivity zone in Bihar would be completely unable to access their 30-day OJT content.

IndiaMART example: An NSD L1 rep completes day 12 of their OJT shadowing session in a rural market in Rajasthan with zero connectivity. On Disprz, they access the pre-downloaded "Shadowing Checklist" and "Lead Qualification" modules, mark their activities, and the data syncs to the LMS that evening when they get hotel Wi-Fi — visible to their manager the next morning.
✓ Disprz ~ SAP Litmos ~ Docebo ~ Cornerstone OD ~ Absorb LMS ~ SAP SuccessFactors ~ Cornerstone LXP ~ TalentLMS ✗ Workday/Sana ✗ Learnyst/Graphy
🧠
Feature 3 · Detailed Assessment Behaviour Tracking
Question-level analytics, error aggregation
Beyond pass/fail scores, IndiaMART's L&D team needs to know which specific questions employees are getting wrong, across which branches, and at what frequency. This powers the Knowledge Ninja Assessment for CSD/KCD teams, and helps identify systemic knowledge gaps after DEAL sessions.

Why partial platforms fall short: Workday/Sana tracks learner performance but its assessment analytics are primarily surfaced through its own AI lens rather than exportable question-level data — the L&D team can see scores but not drill down to "Q4 on WebERP Ticket Handling fails 62% of Bengaluru CSD branch" without custom reporting. Learnyst/Graphy have basic quiz pass/fail only.

IndiaMART example: After a Knowledge Ninja Assessment for the CSD vertical, the platform reveals that "Initial RCA" questions from Extended Shubharambh have a 58% failure rate across three branches. The L&D team triggers a targeted DEAL session specifically for those three branches on the RCA module — without this insight, the problem would be invisible.
✓ SAP Litmos ✓ Docebo ✓ Cornerstone OD ✓ Absorb LMS ✓ SAP SuccessFactors ✓ Cornerstone LXP ✓ TalentLMS ✓ Disprz ~ Workday/Sana ~ Learnyst/Graphy
Category 2 · Content Management
📁
Feature 4 · Multi-format Upload (Video, PPT, PDF, Links)
Including offline session recordings
IndiaMART's T&D team produces content in many formats: PPT decks for Shubharambh sessions, PDF guides for FCP and Sales Process, MP4 recordings of TTT meets and WSB meets, and YouTube/external video links for Webinar content. All platforms must support this diverse content library natively.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is partial: These platforms support videos and PDFs for course creators but have limited support for SCORM packages, external link-based modules, or blended content pipelines. They are built for ed-tech instructors, not enterprise L&D teams managing hundreds of mixed-format modules across 6 verticals.

IndiaMART example: The L&D team uploads the recording of a TTT meet held in Noida, the PowerPoint used in that session, and a PDF handout on "Coaching Techniques" — all linked in a single module. WSB meets are recorded, uploaded, and auto-notified to employees who missed the live session.
✓ Workday/Sana ✓ SAP Litmos ✓ Docebo ✓ Cornerstone OD ✓ Absorb LMS ✓ SAP SuccessFactors ✓ Cornerstone LXP ✓ TalentLMS ✓ Disprz ~ Learnyst/Graphy
🎬
Feature 5 · Video Hosting Integration (Vimeo/Wistia + % API)
Percentage-complete tracking via external video APIs
Uploading a video to the LMS is different from knowing how much of it a learner actually watched. This feature requires the LMS to integrate with external video hosts (Vimeo, Wistia) via their APIs to pull back watch percentage data — so a learner who watched 23% of a 45-minute WSB recording can be marked "incomplete" and nudged to finish.

Why most platforms are partial: Workday/Sana, SAP Litmos, Cornerstone OD, Absorb, SAP SuccessFactors, Cornerstone LXP, TalentLMS, Disprz can embed Vimeo/Wistia videos but rely on the learner clicking a "Mark Complete" button, or use iFrame tracking which is unreliable. Percentage-via-API requires a native connector, which only Docebo has matured.

IndiaMART example: A WSB meet recording is hosted on Vimeo. Docebo's Vimeo connector pulls back that a Bengaluru rep watched only 15% of the 40-minute session. Their manager is automatically flagged, and the rep is re-notified to complete it before the next branch KPI review.

Category 3 · Administration & Authoring
✏️
Feature 6 · Rich WYSIWYG Course Editor + Version Control
Clone modules, templates, admin edits
IndiaMART's Shubharambh induction exists in multiple variants — NSD (5 days), CSD/KCD (4 days + 1 extended day), Tele (4 days + 6 extended days), MPBI (3 days + 5.5 Tech Shubharambh). The L&D team needs to clone a base module and customise for each vertical without rebuilding from scratch, and maintain version control so older cohorts continue seeing the original content.

Why SAP Litmos and TalentLMS are partial: Their built-in editors support basic content creation but version control is less robust — there's no true clone-and-branch workflow. Admins often resort to duplicating courses manually without a linked version history, making it hard to roll back changes.

IndiaMART example: The L&D team updates the "WebERP Module" in Extended Shubharambh after a product update. Version control ensures the Oct 2024 cohort's training record still references the v1 content they completed, while Nov 2024 onwards gets v2 — preventing compliance audit confusion.

Category 4 · Progress Tracking
📈
Feature 7 · Real-time Learner Progress + Manager Dashboards
% complete, time spent, completion deadlines
Branch managers and L&D coordinators need a live view of who has completed what — especially during time-bound programmes like Shubharambh (5 days), OJT (30 days), and FTM (quarterly). Managers should be able to filter by branch, vertical, level, and programme.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is partial: These platforms offer basic learner progress views for individual instructors, but lack the hierarchical multi-branch manager dashboard that an enterprise like IndiaMART needs — where a Sales L5 in Delhi needs to see completion data across 20 reps in 3 different branch locations simultaneously.

IndiaMART example: An NSD Area Manager sees on their Disprz dashboard that 14/20 reps have completed Day 3 of Shubharambh. The 6 incomplete reps are highlighted with their last login time. One rep hasn't logged in for 2 days — the system has already auto-sent an escalation email.

Category 5 · Personalised Learning Paths
🗺️
Feature 8 · Role/Level/Dept Auto-Assignment ★★
Rules-based or AI-driven course assignment per segment
IndiaMART has arguably the most complex vertical segmentation of any Indian B2B tech company: Sales splits into NSD (field), CSD, KCD, and Tele (TA/TM/BD/WinBack) — each with different Shubharambh durations and OJT paths. MPBI splits into Engineering (campus vs lateral), Product (campus vs lateral), QA, and Category — each with unique FOB paths. TTT meets are only visible to L3+ employees; ELDP requires 3-year ratings of 4+; IMElevate targets only "hipot L1s."

Why SAP Litmos and TalentLMS are partial: Both support basic group-based assignment, but the conditional rule engine is limited. Creating a rule like "assign ELDP only if employee.yearsAtCompany ≥ 3 AND employee.avgRating ≥ 4 AND employee.level IN [L3,L4,L5]" requires custom scripting rather than a GUI-driven rule builder, which limits the L&D team's ability to manage it without IT support.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is a hard no: No concept of org hierarchy, roles, or auto-assignment. Every course assignment is manual — completely unscalable for IndiaMART's 5,000+ employee base across 6 verticals.

IndiaMART example: When an NSD L1 employee is promoted to L2, Cornerstone OD automatically un-enrolls them from L1 paths and enrols them into TTT preparation content, the WSB facilitation guide, and flags them for consideration in the next IMElevate cohort — triggered purely by the level change in the HRMS feed.
✓ Workday/Sana ✓ Docebo ✓ Cornerstone OD ✓ Absorb LMS ✓ SAP SuccessFactors ✓ Cornerstone LXP ✓ Disprz ~ SAP Litmos ~ TalentLMS ✗ Learnyst/Graphy

Feature 9 · Personal Employee Dashboard
Individual learner view — own data only
Every IndiaMART employee — from an NSD L1 field rep to a Senior Engineer — needs a clean, private dashboard showing their own learning journey: what they've completed, what's pending, certifications earned (ILEAP, ELDP), and upcoming sessions. This creates ownership of learning and reduces dependency on managers to chase completions.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is partial: Learner dashboards exist but are designed for external students purchasing courses — not internal employees with org-hierarchy context. There is no "my pending mandatory training" view or notification integration with HR systems.

IndiaMART example: An MPBI Engineer logs into their dashboard and sees: Shubharambh complete ✓, Tech Shubharambh complete ✓, FOB-Common 6/8 modules done, AI Tech Session for March not yet watched, ILEAP application deadline in 7 days. Everything in one view, private to them.

Feature 10 · Built-in Quiz Engine + LTI Integrations (Kahoot)
Grade pass-back, survey tools
IndiaMART uses quizzing at multiple touchpoints: Knowledge Ninja Assessments for CSD/KCD branch performance measurement, post-session quizzes after TTT meets, and Shubharambh day-end assessments. The L&D team also uses interactive tools like Kahoot during live sessions — the LMS should integrate with LTI-compliant tools and pass grades back automatically.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is partial: Basic multiple-choice quizzes are supported, but LTI integration for external tools (Kahoot, Mentimeter) and grade pass-back pipelines are absent. The L&D team would need to manually enter Kahoot scores into the LMS — defeating the purpose of integration.

IndiaMART example: During an FTM session in Hyderabad, the trainer runs a Kahoot quiz on leadership scenarios. Feature 11 · AI Quiz / Question Generator
Auto-generate questions from uploaded content
The L&D team at IndiaMART frequently adds new content — product update DEAL sessions, Tech Learning recordings, AI Tech sessions on GenAI and prompt engineering. Manually creating quiz questions for every session is time-consuming. AI quiz generators automatically extract key concepts from uploaded videos or documents and suggest question banks.

Why Workday/Sana leads: Sana is AI-native and can generate full assessments, course outlines, and adaptive quizzes directly from content — the most mature implementation. Disprz also offers strong AI quiz generation. Most other platforms (SAP Litmos, Cornerstone, Absorb, SAP SuccessFactors) have added AI quiz generation as a beta or limited feature, not production-ready.

Why TalentLMS and Learnyst/Graphy have none: No AI content tooling at all — quiz creation is fully manual.

IndiaMART example: After uploading the March AI Tech Session recording (45 minutes, covering prompt engineering basics), the LMS auto-generates 8 quiz questions with IndiaMART-specific distractors. The L&D admin reviews, edits 2 questions, and publishes — what would take 2 hours takes 15 minutes.

Due dates, upcoming sessions, deadline reminders
IndiaMART's training calendar is dense: Shubharambh every joining batch, WSB meets every Wednesday, TTT meets for L3+, IMElevate quarterly, FTM quarterly, DEAL sessions ad hoc. Employees need timely LMS reminders and managers need deadline escalation alerts — all automated, not manually emailed.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is partial: Basic email notifications on course assignment exist, but LMS calendar integration (showing training schedules inside the learner app), escalation rules (e.g., "send manager alert if course not started 48 hours before deadline"), and bulk scheduling are absent.

IndiaMART example: Every Tuesday at 4pm, all CSD/KCD employees receive an automated LMS push notification: "WSB Meet tomorrow at 10am — pre-read the DEAL session from last week." Feature 13 · AI Search, Recommendations & Content Auto-Tagging ★
Course outline generators, adaptive learning tutors
With 6 verticals, 20+ named programmes, and dozens of modules, employees need to be able to find content quickly and receive intelligent recommendations — not scroll through a flat catalogue. AI search should understand natural language queries like "show me what I need for FTM preparation" and surface the right modules.

Why TalentLMS and Learnyst/Graphy have none: Basic keyword search only, with no semantic understanding or recommendation engine. An NSD L2 employee would see everything in the catalogue with no personalised filtering.

Why SAP Litmos, Absorb, and SAP SuccessFactors are partial: These platforms have added AI features but they are primarily in the recommendation layer — not the full suite of content auto-tagging, course outline generation, and adaptive tutors that Workday/Sana and Disprz offer.

IndiaMART example: An L3 CSD employee who just passed IMElevate gets an AI-triggered recommendation: "Based on your IMElevate completion and L3 designation, you may be eligible for FTM — here's what to expect." Feature 14 · Dept/Location/Level Segmented Access + Certifications
Audit trails, compliance tracking
IndiaMART's complex org requires granular access control: TTT content is L3+ only, ELDP requires a 3-year rating of 4+, Gallup Strength Based Training requires L5+ certification, and QA Tester Meets are MPBI-only. A CSD L1 employee should never see Engineering FOB content. The LMS must enforce these walls automatically and maintain audit trails for compliance.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is partial: Basic "groups" exist but there is no multi-level org hierarchy enforcement — you can't create rules that combine level + location + vertical + eligibility criteria. Audit trail export for compliance is also absent.

IndiaMART example: Gallup Strength Based Training is restricted to L5+ employees with a valid Gallup certificate (imported from the third-party Gallup system via API). When an employee reaches L5, the system auto-checks their profile for a Gallup certificate and only then unlocks the training. Without the certificate, the course is invisible — not just locked.

Feature 15 · GDPR/ISO Compliance, Data Encryption, Uptime SLA
Enterprise-grade security for employee data
IndiaMART handles personal data for thousands of employees including performance ratings, assessment scores, and attendance logs — all of which are sensitive HR data. The LMS must comply with applicable data protection standards, encrypt data at rest and in transit, and guarantee high availability SLAs (99.9%+) to not disrupt business-critical induction programmes like Shubharambh.

Why TalentLMS is partial: TalentLMS offers GDPR compliance tools and SSL encryption, but its SLA tiers and audit-level certifications (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001) are less formally documented compared to enterprise-grade competitors. For a large org like IndiaMART, this requires explicit contractual verification.

Why Learnyst/Graphy has none: These are SaaS course-creator platforms not designed for enterprise-grade security compliance. No documented SOC 2, no formal DPA, and no uptime SLA guarantees at enterprise scale — a dealbreaker for IndiaMART's IT and legal teams.

IndiaMART example: IndiaMART's L&D and IT security teams require a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and SOC 2 Type II audit report before going live. Disprz, Docebo, and Cornerstone all provide these; Learnyst/Graphy cannot.

Feature 16 · Custom Report Builder + API Export + Scheduled Reports ★
Multi-dimensional filtering, automated dashboards
IndiaMART's L&D team needs to report on training impact across multiple dimensions: completion rates per branch (for NSD WSB meets), assessment performance per vertical (for Knowledge Ninja), ELDP eligibility tracking by rating (for succession planning), and ILEAP certificate counts per team. These reports need to be scheduled and auto-emailed to HRBPs and VPs.

Why TalentLMS is partial: Good basic reporting but custom dimensions (filtering by branch + level + programme + date range simultaneously) require the premium plan and still lack true API scheduling.

Why Learnyst/Graphy has none: No custom report builder. Data is only visible in the admin panel as basic aggregate counts — no export API, no scheduled delivery, no multi-dimensional filter.

IndiaMART example: The VP of Sales receives an automated Monday morning email report: "Last week's WSB Meet completion — Delhi: 87%, Mumbai: 92%, Bengaluru: 74%, Chennai: 68%. Branches below 75% have been auto-notified." This report is built once in Docebo and runs every Monday without L&D admin involvement.

On Cornerstone OD's base product, they may encounter a multi-level navigation tree before reaching the same content.

Feature 18 · Scalability — 1000+ Concurrent Users, Multi-lingual
No slowdown at enterprise scale
IndiaMART runs large-batch Shubharambh inductions — entire joining cohorts for multiple verticals starting simultaneously. On Day 1 of a large Sales batch, hundreds of employees across India may be accessing the LMS concurrently, watching videos, submitting quizzes, and logging attendance. The platform must not degrade under load.

Why TalentLMS is partial: TalentLMS handles mid-market workloads well (up to ~1,000 users) but at IndiaMART's enterprise scale (5,000+ employees, high-concurrency induction days), performance guarantees and SLA tiers are less robust than the enterprise platforms. It's a great fit for smaller deployments, less so for all-company simultaneous training.

Why Learnyst/Graphy is a hard no: These platforms are built for individual course creators with hundreds of students, not enterprise cohorts of thousands. Concurrent access at IndiaMART's scale would overwhelm the infrastructure — and there is no enterprise SLA to protect against this.

IndiaMART example: On the first day of a 200-person joining cohort in January (across NSD, CSD, Engineering, and Product), all 200 employees simultaneously open Shubharambh Day 1 at 9am. Enterprise platforms like Disprz, SAP SuccessFactors, and Cornerstone handle this with no perceptible slowdown. Learnyst/Graphy would fail.

Feature 19 · Forums, Peer Learning, Leaderboards & Badges
Social learning + gamification for engagement

Feature 20 · QR Code / Geolocation / Badge Scan Attendance ★★
Digital attendance for offline and online sessions on LMS
This is IndiaMART's most differentiating LMS requirement and the single biggest reason to consider Disprz above all others. Shubharambh (5-day induction for every vertical), FTM (city-specific offline sessions quarterly), WSB Wednesday meets (branch-level weekly), and TTT meets all require in-person attendance to be captured digitally and fed directly into the LMS — currently managed via spreadsheets, which is error-prone and creates reconciliation work.

Why most platforms are partial: SAP Litmos, Docebo, Cornerstone OD, Absorb, SAP SuccessFactors, Cornerstone LXP, TalentLMS all support Instructor-Led Training (ILT) session management — the trainer can mark attendance manually in the platform. However, QR code self-scan (employees scan on arrival), geolocation check-in (geo-confirm physical presence for field visits), and badge scan are either third-party integrations or entirely absent. This means the bottleneck is still the trainer manually marking 20–50 people, which is time-consuming and error-prone for FTM sessions in remote cities.
